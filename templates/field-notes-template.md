# Field Notes

> *Documentation is an act of kindness to your Future Self.*

Field Notes capture the journey while it is still unfolding.

They are not polished reports.

They are working notes.

Record what happened, why it happened and what you discovered along the way.

Future You should be able to reconstruct both the process and the reasoning behind it.

# Actions

Describe what you did.

Focus on meaningful actions rather than every individual click.

---

# Observations

What did you notice?

Include both expected and unexpected behaviour.

---

# Decisions

Record important decisions.

For each one, explain:

- What was decided?
- Why was this decision made?
- Which alternatives were considered?
- Under what circumstances should this decision be revisited?

---

# Problems Encountered

Describe the challenges you faced.

Include:

- Symptoms
- Possible causes
- Investigation
- Resolution

If the problem remains unresolved, document the current state and any next steps.

---

# Evidence

Record anything that may help verify or reproduce the work.

Examples:

- Screenshots
- Terminal output
- Configuration files
- Diagrams
- Links
- References

---

# Changes Made

Summarize the meaningful changes introduced during this phase.

---

# Open Questions

Document uncertainties that require further investigation.

Questions are valuable.

Do not remove them simply because they remain unanswered.

---

# Next Steps

What should happen next?

What would you recommend to Future You before continuing?

---

# Reflection

Finish with one short reflection.

Examples:

- What surprised me?
- What became clearer?
- What would I do differently next time?
- Which pattern did I recognise?
- Which note will Future Me appreciate the most?
