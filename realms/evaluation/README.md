# Evaluation

## The Question

> **Did this achieve its purpose?**

Building a solution is only part of the journey.

Evaluation asks whether that solution fulfills the objective it was created to achieve.

It compares intention with outcome and turns experience into improvement.

---

## Purpose

The Evaluation Realm helps you determine whether your work accomplished what it set out to do.

It examines results, identifies strengths and weaknesses and reveals opportunities for refinement.

Evaluation is not about judging success or failure.

It is about understanding outcomes well enough to improve future work.

---

## What Belongs Here?

Examples include:

- testing against requirements
- acceptance criteria
- retrospectives
- performance measurements
- benchmarking
- usability feedback
- code reviews
- project reviews
- quality assurance
- lessons learned
- success metrics
- post-implementation reviews

If it helps determine whether an objective was achieved, it belongs here.

---

## What Does Not Belong Here?

The Evaluation Realm does not primarily explain:

- which components exist;
- how components relate to one another;
- how information moves;
- how ideas are represented;
- what knowledge should be preserved.

Those questions belong to other Realms.

---

## Typical Questions

- Did this solve the original problem?
- Did it meet the defined requirements?
- What worked well?
- What could be improved?
- Were there unintended consequences?
- What evidence supports this conclusion?
- Which assumptions proved correct?
- Which assumptions were wrong?
- What should change next time?
- Is the solution ready to move forward?

---

## Typical Artifacts

Examples include:

- evaluation reports

- test summaries
- benchmarking results
- review notes
- quality reports
- project reflections
- improvement plans
- completion reports

---

## Relationships with Other Realms

Evaluation draws upon every other Realm.

It considers:

- whether the identified systems fulfilled their purpose;
- whether the relationships supported the solution;
- whether communication was effective;
- whether trust was justified;
- whether the chosen forms of expression improved understanding; and
- which knowledge should be carried into future Adventures.

Evaluation completes the current Adventure while preparing the next one.

---

## The Goal

Every Adventure should leave you with more than a finished project.

It should leave you with a clearer understanding of what succeeded, what could improve and how that insight will shape the journey ahead.
