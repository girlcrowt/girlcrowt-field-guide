# Relationships

## The Question

> **How do the parts influence one another?**

A list of components does not yet describe a functioning system.

Systems emerge through relationships.

The Relationships Realm examines how individual parts depend on, support, constrain and affect one another.

---

## Purpose

The Relationships Realm helps you understand how components are connected and what those connections mean.

It asks how a change in one part of the system may affect another.

This perspective reveals dependencies, responsibilities, structure and consequences that remain invisible when components are examined in isolation.

---

## What Belongs Here?

Examples include:

- dependencies
- hierarchies
- ownership
- membership
- associations
- cardinalities
- parent-child structures
- client-server relationships
- service dependencies
- user-group membership
- database relationships
- resource assignments
- trust relationships
- technical and human responsibilities

If one part of a system affects, depends upon or belongs to another, that connection belongs here.

---

## What Does Not Belong Here?

The Relationships Realm does not primarily explain:

- which components exist;
- how information is transmitted;
- whether access should be permitted;
- how ideas are represented;
- whether the completed solution meets its requirements.

Those questions belong to other Realms.

---

## Typical Questions

- How are the components connected?
- Which parts depend on one another?
- What belongs to what?
- Who is responsible for what?
- Which relationships are mandatory?
- Which relationships are optional?
- What happens when one component changes?
- What happens when one component disappears?
- Which relationships create risk?
- Which relationships create resilience?
- Where are relationships missing, unclear or unnecessarily complex?

---

Examples include:

- responsibility assignments
- service maps
- parent-child hierarchies
- access mappings
- data models
- dependency inventories

---

## Relationships with Other Realms

The Systems Realm identifies the parts.

The Relationships Realm explains how those parts affect one another.

Other Realms build upon that understanding.

Examples:

- Communication examines how information moves across relationships.
- Trust examines whether a relationship should be permitted or relied upon.
- Knowledge preserves why relationships exist and how they should be maintained.
- Evaluation examines whether the relationships support the intended purpose.

---

## The Goal

Understanding individual components is not enough.

You must also understand what connects them.

A system becomes meaningful through the relationships between its parts.
