# Knowledge

## The Question

> **What should be preserved?**

Experience becomes valuable when it can be remembered, shared and built upon.

The Knowledge Realm explores what understanding should endure beyond a single task, lesson or Adventure.

Its purpose is not to collect information.

Its purpose is to preserve understanding that remains useful over time.

---

## Purpose

The Knowledge Realm helps you identify, organize and preserve what is worth carrying forward.

It asks which ideas, principles and experiences deserve a permanent place in the Field Guide.

Knowledge is not measured by how much is collected.

It is measured by how much remains meaningful.

---

## What Belongs Here?

Examples include:

- concepts
- principles
- patterns
- best practices
- documentation
- reference material
- cheat sheets
- playbooks
- design decisions
- engineering practices
- lessons learned
- reusable solutions
- shared vocabulary

If it helps future understanding, it belongs here.

---

## What Does Not Belong Here?

The Knowledge Realm does not primarily explain:

- which components exist;
- how components interact;
- how information moves;
- how ideas are represented;
- whether a solution fulfills its purpose.

Those questions belong to other Realms.

## Relationships with Other Realms

Every Realm contributes knowledge.

The Knowledge Realm decides what should endure.

Examples:

- Systems identify what exists.
- Relationships explain how those parts connect.
- Communication explains how information moves.
- Trust determines what evidence deserves confidence.
- Expression shapes how knowledge is represented.
- Evaluation determines whether new understanding is complete.

Knowledge preserves the understanding that future Adventures will build upon.

---

## The Goal

Learning is not the accumulation of information.

It is the preservation of understanding that remains useful long after the details have faded.
