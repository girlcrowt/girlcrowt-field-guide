# Prepare the Host

## Mission Call Sheet

**Objective**

Prepare the host computer for the Lab by verifying that it satisfies the requirements defined during Training.

---

**Inputs**

- Lab Requirements Profile
- Toolkit Decision Record
- Recovery Strategy

---

**Outputs**

A host system that is ready for the Lab.

---

**Estimated Effort**

Moderate

---

**Difficulty**

Beginner

---

**Primary Realms**

Systems • Trust

---

**Engineering Principles**

Assumptions are hypotheses until verified.

Preparation reduces uncertainty.

Trust is earned through verification.

---

**Success Criteria**

- [ ] Hardware-assisted virtualization is supported.
- [ ] Hardware-assisted virtualization is enabled.
- [ ] Available system resources satisfy the documented requirements.
- [ ] The host operating system is ready for deployment.
- [ ] The Recovery Strategy has been reviewed.
- [ ] All assumptions have been verified or updated.

---

**World State Updated**

- Host system verified.
- Deployment may begin.

---

## Phase Brief

The Lab will live on your existing computer.

Before introducing new software, you must confirm that your host system is capable of supporting the environment you designed during Training.

Do not assume.

Verify.

If reality differs from your design, update the design before continuing.

A corrected plan is always better than forcing reality to fit an incorrect assumption.

Don't forget to document any changes you may make!

---

## The Goal

By the end of this phase, you should know with confidence that your host system is ready to support your Lab.

If it is not, this is the right moment to adapt your design.

Preparation is part of engineering.

Finding a problem before deployment is a success, not a setback.

---

## Checkpoint

Before continuing to Phase 02, ask yourself:

- Does my host system satisfy my documented requirements?
- Have I verified every critical assumption?
- Do I understand any limitations that still exist?
- Is my Recovery Strategy still appropriate?
- Am I confident enough to begin deployment?

If the answer to any of these questions is "No", update your documentation before moving on.

The expedition continues when the foundation is ready.
