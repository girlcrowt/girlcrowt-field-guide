## Phase Brief

Building the Lab was never the final goal.

Building a Lab you can trust was.

Validation confirms that your work reflects your original intentions and that your documentation tells the same story as your system.

A successful Mission is not measured by how quickly it was completed.

It is measured by how confidently you can continue building upon it.

---

## The Goal

Evaluate your Lab against the work completed throughout this Adventure.

Rather than searching for perfection, confirm that the Lab is stable, understandable and ready to support future exploration.

---

## Suggested Workflow

### Review

Compare your completed Lab with your Mission Plan and Lab Requirements Profile.

---

### Validate

Confirm that every Success Criterion has been met.

Where differences exist, decide whether the system or the documentation should be updated.

---

### Verify

Test your recovery process.

Confirm that backups, snapshots and documentation work together as intended.

---

### Reflect

Record any improvements you would make if you built the Lab again.

Every completed Adventure improves the next one.

---

## Checkpoint

Before closing Adventure 01, ask yourself:

- Does my Lab fullfil its intended purpose?
- Can I explain how every major component contributes to the system?
- Could I recover from a significant mistake?
- Would another learner understand this Lab using only my documentation?
- Am I confident enough to build my next Adventure on this foundation?

If not, improve the Lab before declaring the Mission complete.

Remember: Validation builds trust.
